Syracuse and Ephesus being at variance , there was a cruel law made at Ephesus , ordaining that if any merchant of Syracuse was seen in the city of Ephesus he was to be put to death , unless he could pay a thousand marks for the ransom of his life . Aegeon , an old merchant of Syracuse , was discovered in the streets of Ephesus , and brought before the duke , either to pay this heavy fine or receive sentence of death .
