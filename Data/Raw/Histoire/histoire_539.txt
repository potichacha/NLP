Regan and her husband were keeping their court in great pomp and state at their palace ; and Lear despatched his servant Caius with letters to his daughter , that she might be prepared for his reception , while he and his train followed after . But it seems that Goneril had been beforehand with him , sending letters also to Regan , accusing her father of waywardness and ill-humors , and advising her not to receive so great a train as he was bringing with him .
