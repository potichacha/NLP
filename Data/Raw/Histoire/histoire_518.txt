Lear , whom he had honored as a king , loved as a father , followed as a master ; and he had never esteemed his life further than as a pawn to wage against his royal master 's enemies , nor feared to lose it when Lear 's safety was the motive ; nor , now that Lear was most his own enemy , did this faithful servant of the king forget his old principles , but manfully opposed Lear to do Lear good ; and was unmannerly only because Lear was mad . He had been a most faithful counselor in times past
