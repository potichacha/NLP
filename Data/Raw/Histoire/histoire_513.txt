If she should ever wed , she was sure the lord to whom she gave her husband would want half her love , half of her care and duty ; she should never marry like her sisters , to love her father all . Cordelia , who in earnest loved her old father even almost extravagantly as her sisters pretended to do , would have plainly told him so at any other time , in more daughter-like and loving terms
