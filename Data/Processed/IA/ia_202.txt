how can i develop a habit of drawing daily

Developing a habit of drawing daily can be a great way to improve your skills and express your creativity. Here are some tips to help you build this habit: Set a specific goal: Decide on a specific goal for your daily drawing practice. For example, you may want to draw for a certain amount of time each day or aim to complete a certain number of drawings each week. Create a routine: Choose a specific time of day that works for you and stick to it. You may want to set a reminder on your phone or put it on your calendar to make sure you don't forget. Start small: Begin with a manageable amount of time each day, such as 10-15 minutes, and gradually increase the amount of time as you become more comfortable with the habit. Make it enjoyable: Choose subjects or styles that you enjoy drawing, and experiment with different techniques and materials to keep your practice interesting. Track your progress: Keep track of your progress by taking pictures of your work, keeping a sketchbook or journal, or sharing your drawings with others. Find a community: Join a drawing group or community to connect with others who share your passion and get inspiration and feedback on your work. Remember, developing a daily drawing habit takes time and effort, but it can be a rewarding and fulfilling experience. Keep at it, and you will see improvement in your skills and creativity over time.
