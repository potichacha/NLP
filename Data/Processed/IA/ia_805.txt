The BMC is composed of nine building blocks that describe the key aspects of a business model: Customer Segments: The specific groups of customers that the business aims to serve. Value Proposition: The unique value that the business offers to its customers. Channels: The methods used to reach and communicate with customers. Customer Relationships: The ways in which the business interacts with its customers. Revenue Streams: The sources of revenue for the business. Key Activities: The most important tasks required to deliver the value proposition. Key Resources: The essential resources required to create, deliver and capture value. Key Partnerships: The important relationships and alliances required to execute the business model. Cost Structure: The costs associated with delivering the value proposition.
