when did Confucian ideas start in Japan?

Confucian ideas were first introduced to Japan in the 5th century, but they did not become widely accepted until the 17th century. During the Edo period (1603-1868), Confucianism became a major influence on Japanese society, particularly among the ruling samurai class. Confucian teachings emphasized the importance of moral values, education, and social hierarchy, and had a significant impact on Japanese philosophy, ethics, and social norms. Confucianism also played a role in shaping Japan's political and economic systems during this period.
